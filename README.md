**Question - 1: How would you differentiate between the mission statement and the objective statement? Compare and contrast them.**

**A:**

*   **Mission Statement:** This is the company's **purpose** and **reason for existing**. It describes what the company does, who it serves, and its core values. It is broad, inspirational, and doesn't change often.
    *   *Example for Pine Valley Furniture: "To create high-quality, handcrafted furniture that turns houses into homes."*

*   **Objective Statement:** These are the **specific, measurable goals** the company sets to achieve its mission. They are concrete targets with a deadline. They change as goals are met or strategies evolve.
    *   *Example for Pine Valley Furniture: "Increase online sales by 25% within two years" or "Launch three new sustainable product lines by the end of next year."*

**Comparison:**

| Feature | Mission Statement | Objective Statement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Purpose** | Defines **WHY** the company exists | Defines **WHAT** it wants to achieve |
| **Nature** | Broad, philosophical, and permanent | Specific, measurable, and time-bound |
| **Focus** | On the present identity and scope | On future targets and success |

**Question - 3: Discuss the evaluation criteria used in classifying and ranking projects. How do these criteria impact the decision-making process for project selection?**

**A:**

**Evaluation Criteria:**
Projects are typically ranked based on a few key criteria:

1.  **Value/Benefits:** What is the return on investment (ROI)? Will it increase profits, save costs, or improve customer satisfaction?
2.  **Cost:** How much money is needed to complete the project?
3.  **Risk:** What is the chance of the project failing? Are there technical, financial, or market risks?
4.  **Alignment with Goals:** Does the project help the company achieve its main mission and strategic objectives?

**Impact on Decision-Making:**
These criteria act as a filter to help managers make smart choices.

*   They help compare very different projects on the same scale.
*   Managers can prioritize projects that offer the **highest value** for the **lowest cost and risk**.
*   It ensures that the company only invests in projects that directly support its main goals, avoiding wasted time and money on unimportant ideas.

**Question - 4: Explain the differences between top-down and bottom-up planning approaches and express your preference for one, with appropriate justification.**

**A:**

*   **Top-Down Planning:**
    *   **What it is:** Senior management creates the plans and goals, which are then passed down for lower levels to execute.
    *   **Pros:** Fast, aligned with overall company strategy, clear direction.
    *   **Cons:** Can lack input from employees doing the actual work, which may lead to unrealistic plans or lower morale.

*   **Bottom-Up Planning:**
    *   **What it is:** Lower-level employees and departments create plans based on their day-to-day experience, which are then combined into a larger company plan.
    *   **Pros:** More practical, detailed, and often more accurate. Increases employee buy-in.
    *   **Cons:** Can be slow and may lack strategic alignment if not properly coordinated.

**My Preference and Justification:**
I prefer a **hybrid approach** that combines the best of both.

*   **Justification:** Senior management should set the overall **strategic goals** (top-down) to ensure everyone is moving in the same direction. Then, individual teams should be involved in figuring out the **specific steps and details** to achieve those goals (bottom-up). This ensures the plan is both strategically sound and practically feasible.

**Question - 5: Suppose your boss has assigned you to identify and select information system development projects in your organization. Now identify the possible evaluation criteria that will help you to classify and rank the project.**

**A:**
To classify and rank the projects, I would use these evaluation criteria:

1.  **Strategic Alignment:** How well does the project support the company's main business goals?
2.  **Cost/Benefit Analysis:** What is the expected Return on Investment (ROI)? Will the benefits (increased sales, cost savings) outweigh the costs?
3.  **Technical Feasibility:** Do we have the technology and skills to successfully complete the project?
4.  **Risk Level:** What is the chance of failure? Are the risks (technical, financial, operational) manageable?
5.  **Urgency & Timeframe:** How quickly do we need this project? Can it be delivered in a reasonable time?
6.  **Resource Availability:** Do we have the budget, staff, and time to dedicate to this project?

**Question - 6: Imagine you're a project manager planning to improve a worldwide supply chain system. Your goal is to pick the best IT project. Integrate and prioritize essential factors to ensure smooth integration across all their global operations.**

**A:**
To pick the best IT project for a global supply chain, I would prioritize the factors in this order:

**1. Strategic Alignment (Most Important):**
*   The project must directly support the key business goal of improving the **worldwide supply chain**. Any project that doesn't align with this core objective should be rejected.

**2. Global Integration & Compatibility:**
*   The new system **must work well** with all existing technology across different countries and regions. A system that creates silos or doesn't connect with regional platforms will fail.

**3. Cost-Effectiveness:**
*   The project should offer a strong value proposition. We will calculate the ROI based on expected savings from improved efficiency, reduced delays, and lower inventory costs.

**4. Scalability and Flexibility:**
*   The solution must be able to grow and adapt to future changes in the global market, such as entering new countries or handling increased product volume.

**5. Risk Management:**
*   We will assess and prioritize projects with a lower risk of disruption to current operations during the rollout phase. A phased implementation approach is often best.pg
